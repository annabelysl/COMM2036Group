Effects of Disinformation

Disinformation on the internet impacts human rights, the right to think and express one's thoughts freely; the right to privacy; the right to free expression; and the right to engage in public affairs and vote in elections. Disinformation, in general, lowers the quality of democracy. It undermines faith in democratic institutions, distorts election processes, and promotes online incivility and polarization. While strong counter-disinformation is required to safeguard democracy, it has the potential to undermine human rights and democratic quality. But, most importantly, disinformation can affect how we function as a society. We lose trust in each other and the relationships we create in the internet.

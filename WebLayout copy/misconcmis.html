Misconceptions about misinformation

Misinformation is incorrect or misleading information.[1] It differs from disinformation, which is deliberately deceptive. Rumours are information not attributed to any particular source,[5] and so are unreliable and often unverified, but can turn out to be either true or false. Advances in technology has impacted the way we communicate information and the way misinformation is spread. Misinformation has impacts on our societies' ability to receive information which then influences our communities, politics, and medical field.[7]

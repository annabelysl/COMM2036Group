What is fact checking?

There are three essential components of fact checking


a process of investigating a report, news or information to verify whether it is a fact or falsehood
Fact-checking has the potential to prevent people from being deceived by unconfirmed information
Moreover, it is also a practical way for individuals to avoid rumours, fake news, misinformation, and disinformation.

Tools to Fact-Check

How do you actually use them?

Which tools are the best? There are multiple ways to fact-check and ensure that the data you are using are the truest kind. Here are tips on how you can fact-check:

  1. Check the source of the news
  2. Check the date of the information
  3. Check “about us” of the website, find out who writes the article and who is the owner of the website
  4. Be aware of personal prejudice
  5. Be cautious of the author's tone in the text, make sure it is written in an objective tone without any subjective words and biassed opinion
